Pizza

Quick and easy pizza that doesn't need to sit overnight!

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 cup white onions finely diced
  • 2 large cloves garlic minced
  • 14.5 oz can diced tomatoes drained and pureed
  • 1 tablespoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 tablespoon parmesan cheese
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup very warm water
  • 2 teaspoons sugar divided
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour sifted
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 heaping teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 heaping teaspoon parmesan cheese
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • green bell pepper diced
  • black olives
  • pepperoni
  1. Add olive oil into a large skillet over medium heat
  2. Add onions and saut√© until tender.
  3. Stir in garlic and cook just until fragrant
  4. Stir in pureed tomatoes, Italian seasoning, parmesan cheese, brown sugar, and red pepper flakes.
  5. Slowly stir in chicken broth.
  6. Bring to a light boil and then reduce heat to barely a simmer.
  7. Cook until liquid is reduced to your liking.
  8. To make the crust, in a small bowl or glass, add 1 teaspoon sugar to very warm water.
  9. Stir in yeast and let sit for 10-15 minutes to allow the yeast to proof.
  10. When yeast is ready add mixture to a large bowl.
  11. Stir in flour, salt, remaining sugar, oil, Italian seasoning, and parmesan cheese.
  12. When dough forms, place dough on a lightly floured surface and knead gently with oiled hands.
  13. Press the dough into a lightly greased 12-inch pizza pan. Be sure to keep your hands oiled with olive oil to soften the dough and make it easier to press out.
  14. Cover dough with plastic wrap and place in a warm location for 30 minutes
  15. Preheat oven to 425 F.
  16. When ready to serve, spread sauce over pizza.
  17. Top with cheese and toppings.
  18. Bake for 12 minutes or until the crust in the center of the pizza is fluffy and fully cooked.
  19. Garnish with fresh basil if desired.
